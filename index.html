Phase Goal
Stabilize HRV, sharpen run speed, build swim & bike base
🏃‍♀️ Running strategy: You already have the distance. Running is in maintenance + sharpening mode. Swim and bike are where you're building something new — that's where the adaptation stress lives right now.

3-week taper leading into the May half marathon:

The fitness is already built. Taper is about arriving fresh, not gaining more fitness. Resist the urge to squeeze in extra sessions.

Check your Garmin HRV every morning before deciding on the day's session intensity.

IF: HRV low / red on Garmin
→ Wednesday speed session becomes an easy 4-mile run or is skipped entirely. Never push intervals on a red day.
IF: HRV unstable 3+ days in a row
→ Take an unplanned down week. Cut all volume by 40%. Prioritize sleep above everything.
IF: Easy run HR above 155
→ Stop and walk until it drops. The session is already complete — more miles won't help.
IF: Green HRV + feeling strong
→ Execute the plan as written. Resist the urge to add extra.
😴
Sleep
8–9 hrs. Your #1 performance lever. Consistent wake time matters as much as duration for HRV.
🥩
Post-workout protein
20–30g within 45 min of any session. Critical after speed work and strength.
☀️
Vitamin D
Take with your fattiest meal for best absorption. Ask your doctor to recheck levels in spring — Chicago winters often need higher doses.
💧
Hydration
Cold weather masks thirst. Drink proactively. Aim for pale yellow urine, especially on long bike days.
🧘
Mobility
10 min post-run hip flexor + hamstring work. Especially important after speed sessions.
